RES — Combining precise pollen detection with actionable insights. Empowering people to breathe easier and live better.

According to statistics, pollen allergies affect one in two people worldwide. But in the whole of London there is only one robust pollen detection machine...

## Background
We interviewed over 20 allergy sufferers, conducted workshops with 10 users, and consulted several professors and industry experts to gather insights on current allergy management challenges. From these conversations, we identified significant frustration with the lack of personalized allergy prevention tools.

## User Research
Customers want effective data-driven methods to manage their allergic symptoms. However, they want to have a minimal way of sensing their triggers, with minimal manual intervention on their part. Customers also have their unique ways of mitigating their symptoms so we considered this in the app experience as well. Feedback from pollen and allergy experts also helped refine our concept technically.

### Stakeholder Workshop
The current pollen measurement system still retains the design of the last century, but here are many drawbacks:

**Inadequate Sensor** — Limited and inadequate distribution of accurate pollen sensors leads to location bias, affecting the reliability of pollen data in many localities.

**Unverifiable Correlation** — Unlike weather data, pollen counts are difficult to verify; low-fidelity sensors can report low pollen levels while individuals still react to allergens, leading to uncertainty in managing symptoms.

**Cognitive Biases** — Even those with severe allergens have cognitive biases around their allergens but lack accurate testing, knowledge or equipment, potentially misidentifying the sources of their reactions.

**No Lifestyle Changes** — Simply providing a forecast on pollen levels may not be enough to encourage lifestyle changes; individuals need predictable, actionable data and solutions that still consider their preferences.

"Current methods of pollen allergy avoidance are inadequate. With almost any amount of pollen being an allergic trigger... So what if pollen forecasts can be designed to be more localized and effective for allergy avoidance and mitigation?"

## Approach
Our team looked at the issue of allergies from multiple perspectives.
- From a clinical diagnostic and treatment perspective, the primary measure of avoidance seemed to be the best option for treating pollen allergies.
- From a technical perspective, finding an appropriate mode to sample pollen with sufficient accuracy was incredibly important.
- From a patient perspective, knowing when it's safest to travel with a lower risk of exposure and allergic reaction became one more goal for us.

### RES Device
Collecting data from multiple sensors enables better-localized protection. Distributing pollen sensors helps to remove location bias in pollen data. This improves the reach of pollen detection data and can serve to increase understanding of what is in our air.

### RES App
RES app can show ways to avoid pollen or to mitigate their allergies with more bespoke dosages of antihistamine intervention. We did not stop at just the sensor — we consider how we multiply its effective efficacy and range.

### AI Driven
Utilizing AI and API data. AI empowers swift and accurate detection, enhancing immediacy and precision. Distributing pollen sensors helps to remove location bias in pollen data. This improves the reach of pollen detection data and can serve to increase understanding of what is in our air.

### Benefit — Prediction Map
The map displays a simulated RES pollen map over a few hours. Interact with the model with gestures and explore!

### Customized App
Software customizes and constantly learns allergy info, personalized allergy services for individuals.

**Allergy Details** — Everyone not only has the opportunity to see system info, but also self-report at any time, allowing individuals to also become part of the overall system service.

**Allergy Forecast** — Predict in real time when and where you will have an allergic reaction, react in advance. The first time you're exposed to an allergen, tells you when to take the medicine and what dosage.
