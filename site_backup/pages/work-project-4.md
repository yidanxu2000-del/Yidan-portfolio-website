## Project Overview
This is an NFT project, It's a platform where people can freely design their own NFT profile picture and buy NFTS, the design part including NFT avatar, app and Wechat H5 side.

## My Contributions
- NFT avatar design
- Co-create wireframes and complete high fidelity
- Logo and animation design
- Docking programmer and product managers

This was my first project after finishing my internship, working alongside the product manager on an outsourced assignment. The project was a relatively new NFT custom trading platform based in China, mainly targeting young users who want the freedom to personalise and trade their own NFTs.

Back in 2022, NFTs in China were still in their early days — not nearly as developed as in the West. While Western NFTs were often tied to investment and finance, the scene in China was more about fashion, culture, and creative expression. People weren't really keen on spending money on NFTs yet, and the space felt like a blank canvas with very few examples to learn from.

So with that in mind, our team took a "light digital culture" approach. We wanted to tap into the curiosity of young users who love exploring trends. Instead of pushing the profit angle, we focused on customisation, style, and self-expression — making NFTs feel more like digital fashion or collectibles. We also brought the experience into spaces young people already used daily, like WeChat and mini programs. That's what Nspace was all about.

In this my first project working alongside the product manager on an outsourced assignment. The team was small — just four of us: the product manager, a developer, someone handling marketing, and myself. Over the course of a one-month sprint, I took on multiple roles, covering product design, visual design, promotional assets, and illustration work and docking with programmer and product manager. It was a fast-paced, hands-on experience that really pushed my creative and collaborative skills.

### Wireframe — Ports And Functions

**Collectibles Gallery** — The Digital Collectibles page lets users curate and showcase their NFTs. In the Digital Collectibles, users can freely browse all the NFTs they own and even gift them to friends, it is turning digital ownership into a more social and meaningful experience.

**Showcase Your NFTs** — This feature allows users to display their customised NFTs to social media. For many young people in China, NFTs are a way of showing off personal style, almost like digital fashion. So having a card to showcase them is key to encouraging self-expression and identity building.

**NFT Community** — The community feature creates space for users to design their NFTs and share to community, or find other people's designed NFTs and get it through community activities. It gives young users that sense of belonging they're after through trendy collectibles and shared community vibes.

### Color Pattern
#18191E, #1F69FE, #979797

We chose this colour scheme to create a modern, tech-forward, and futuristic vibe, especially for a young Chinese audience in the NFT space. The deep grey and black give a sense of mystery and sophistication, highlighting the innovative and cutting-edge nature of the product. The bright blue adds energy and a digital feel, appealing to those interested in new technology. The light grey helps balance the overall design, keeping it sleek and elegant, while the white adds freshness and clarity, making the other colours stand out. Together, the colours reflect the digital art aspect of NFTs while resonating with the style and innovation that young people are drawn to.

### Visual Style
For the character design, I drew inspiration from some of the most popular street style and pop culture trends in China at the time. Elements like blonde hair, dreadlocks, ear piercings, and visual references to iconic films like Avatar were incorporated to resonate with young users. The goal was to create a look and feel that felt bold, expressive, and instantly relatable — something that reflected their tastes and made the NFTs feel personal and culturally relevant.

### Illustration Style — "Playful"
In December 2022, China's NFT development was still at an early stage — the market remained in a regulatory gray area, with relatively low financial attributes but a strong focus on collectible and trendy cultural elements. This visual system followed a "decentralized, light digital culture" strategy. It intentionally reduced financial visual cues commonly associated with NFTs, aligned with the aesthetics and usage scenarios favored by young users (such as social media, avatars, and the metaverse), and retained a solid foundation for IP scalability and customization.
